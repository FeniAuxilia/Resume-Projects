Is your data safe in the cloud?

In the current age of smart devices and smartphones, any image taken using these devices is immediately auto-uploaded to the cloud (Google Photos, iCloud, etc) or the internet (Social media sites like Facebook, Twitter, etc). And there is an archive of all the contents that are being uploaded to the internet in the Wayback Machine. So, one must be cautious about what is being uploaded to the internet.

Data security in the cloud is a critical concern for organizations that store, process, and manage their data in cloud environments. Cloud computing offers numerous benefits, such as scalability, cost-efficiency, and accessibility, but it also introduces unique security challenges. Loss, misuse, modification, or unauthorized access to sensitive information can adversely affect the privacy or welfare of an individual, trade secrets of a business, or even the security and international relations of a nation depending on the level of sensitivity and nature of the information.

About the project

The primary objective is to create a robust algorithm that can identify various forms of sensitive data in images, such as text, identification numbers, faces, or any other private information. Upon detecting sensitive data, the system will automatically redact or anonymize the data to make it unreadable or unrecognizable while preserving the overall image's context and quality. The project supports multiple types of images, including photographs, scanned documents, screenshots, and more. It is also adaptable to handle different image formats and resolutions.
